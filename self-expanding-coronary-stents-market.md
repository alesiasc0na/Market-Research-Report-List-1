<p><p>Self-expanding coronary stents are a type of medical device designed to treat coronary artery disease by providing support to blood vessels after angioplasty. Unlike balloon-expandable stents, self-expanding stents are made from materials that allow them to gradually expand upon contact with body heat or through mechanical means, improving their adaptability to the arterial walls. This feature can enhance outcomes in complex vascular anatomies, leading to reduced restenosis rates and better patient prognosis.</p><p>The self-expanding coronary stents market is poised for significant growth, expected to rise at a CAGR of 12.40% during the forecast period. This growth is fueled by an increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in stent technology, and a rise in percutaneous coronary interventions (PCI). Additionally, the market is witnessing a trend towards biocompatible and biodegradable materials, which could improve patient safety and outcomes. Technological innovations, such as drug-eluting self-expanding stents, are also gaining traction, enhancing therapeutic efficacy. Furthermore, the aging population and an increase in lifestyle-related health issues contribute to a growing demand for minimally invasive cardiovascular solutions, thereby propelling market expansion.</p></p>
