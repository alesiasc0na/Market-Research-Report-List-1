<p><p>The patient monitor market is categorized into high-acuity, mid-acuity, and low-acuity monitors based on the severity of patient conditions. High-acuity monitors are designed for critically ill patients, offering advanced features for real-time monitoring of vital signs and complex data. Mid-acuity monitors serve patients needing moderate observation, often used in telemetry units and step-down care. Low-acuity monitors are suitable for stable patients in outpatient or routine care settings, focusing on basic vital sign tracking with minimal features.</p></p>
